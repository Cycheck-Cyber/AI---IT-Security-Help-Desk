Why This Matters
I
Raw Alerts Arrive Faster Than You Can Investigate
SIEM alerts, vendor bulletins, CVE feeds, regulatory warnings — they all land in the same overwhelmed inbox, written by specialists for specialists. Each raw alert arrives without context, without priority, without a recommended action. The things that actually matter for your environment slip through.
II
The Expertise You'd Need, You Can't Hire
A full security operations team is out of reach for most mid-sized organisations. Specialists are scarce, expensive, and rarely stay. So the work falls on an IT team already at capacity — one that is brilliant at what it does, but was never meant to be a SOC on top of everything else.
III
The First Walk-Through Shouldn't Be During the Incident
When an incident actually hits, the questions get asked in a rush: Who decides? Who calls legal? What do we tell customers? Teams that rehearse these questions calmly, in advance, handle the real thing ten times better. Teams that don't, learn the hard way — in public.

Domains We Cover

One central desk — across every security area that matters to you

Your environment doesn't split cleanly into silos, so neither does our coverage. One consistent advisory desk, one voice, one relationship — spanning the areas where the real threats actually appear.

Endpoint & Workstation
Ransomware, credential theft, suspicious executables — the threats that land directly on your users' machines.
Identity & Access
Account compromise, phishing, impossible-travel logins — the front door most real attacks walk through.
Network & Firewall
Lateral movement, command-and-control traffic, unusual outbound flows — the signals that show something is already inside.
Cloud & SaaS
Misconfigured storage, over-permissioned identities, shadow SaaS — the modern perimeter you may not fully see.
Email & Collaboration
Business email compromise, invoice fraud, targeted phishing — the attacks that still cost organisations the most.
Supply Chain & Third Party
Vendor breaches, software supply-chain attacks, third-party exposure — the risk that enters through someone else's door.
AI & Emerging Threats
Attacks targeting AI tools, agentic systems, and the new surfaces that traditional advisories don't yet cover well.
Regulatory & Compliance
New rules, new obligations, new evidence requirements — translated into what you actually have to do, and by when.
Operational Resilience
Backup, recovery, business-continuity — the quiet capabilities that decide how bad a bad day actually gets.
