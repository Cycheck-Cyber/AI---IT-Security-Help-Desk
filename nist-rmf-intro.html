The Problem
I
Risk Without Measurement
AI systems are being deployed faster than they can be governed. Boards ask "how risky is our AI?" and receive narrative answers — not numbers. Without a consistent scoring model, risk exposure cannot be compared, tracked, or defended to regulators.
II
Compliance Without Visibility
Spreadsheet-based NIST AI RMF reviews die within weeks of launch. Data is stale, ownership is unclear, and residual risk is invisible to the leadership team. Governance becomes a one-off audit exercise rather than a living programme.
III
Governance Without Evidence
When an incident occurs, organisations need to show they measured, mitigated, and monitored AI risk. A structured, timestamped, auditable assessment trail is the difference between a defensible record and a reputational crisis.
Platform Overview

AI risk, quantified and defensible

The NIST AI RMF Assessment platform converts the full 294-control framework into a guided conversation. Risk officers, compliance leads, and AI product owners work through each function — GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE — answering plain-language questions instead of parsing spreadsheets.

Behind the interface, an Azure OpenAI scoring engine evaluates every answer across four dimensions: Impact, Likelihood, Control Weakness, and Detectability. The result is a residual risk score for every finding — aggregated into a live matrix, a KPI strip, and a defensible audit record.

01
Chat-Driven Interview
The full NIST AI RMF becomes a conversation. No spreadsheets, no training required. Respondents answer plain questions; the platform tracks progress across all four functions automatically.
02
Four-Dimensional AI Scoring
Every answer is scored by an Azure OpenAI model across Impact, Likelihood, Control Weakness, and Detectability — producing a residual risk score on a transparent 0–27 scale.
03
Live Risk Matrix & KPIs
Findings appear in a 4×4 impact/likelihood matrix in real time. Critical, High, Medium, and Low counts update as the assessment progresses — visible to the governance team throughout.
04
Transparent Methodology
Every score is reproducible. The residual formula, thresholds, and worked examples are published in-app — no opaque AI verdicts, no black boxes. Audit-ready by design.
How It Works

From first question to
defensible risk matrix
in five steps

01
Secure Login
Assessors sign in through a single-sign-on portal protected by one-time-password verification. Every session is private to the organisation, with all conversation data transmitted over an encrypted, authenticated channel.
02
Structured Interview
The platform guides the respondent through GOVERN → MAP → MEASURE → MANAGE. Each of the 294 controls is surfaced as a single plain-language question in a chat interface.
03
AI Dimensional Scoring
Azure OpenAI evaluates each answer on four dimensions — Impact, Likelihood, Control Weakness, Detectability — with scores from 0 to 3. Reasoning is captured alongside the scores.
04
Risk Aggregation
A residual risk score is calculated per finding via a published formula. Findings are placed on a 4×4 impact/likelihood matrix and classified as Critical, High, Medium, or Low.
05
Governance Report
On completion, the platform renders a full risk matrix view — KPI strip, top risks, full distribution — persisted in PostgreSQL and available to the governance committee on demand.

Risk Classification

Every finding is placed
on a defensible four-tier scale

Score ≥ 13
Critical
Unacceptable exposure — immediate structural action required. Board visibility, mandatory remediation plan, and short-term compensating controls.
Score 8 – 12
High
Priority remediation required. Documented treatment plan, named owner, and tracked progress through the next two governance cycles.
Score 4 – 7
Medium
Address in the standard governance cycle. Included in the next quarterly review with documented action, owner, and expected completion date.
Score 0 – 3
Low
Acceptable with monitoring. Tracked in the risk register and re-evaluated on the next full assessment — no immediate action required.
The Formula

A transparent scoring model — not a black box

Every residual risk score in the platform is reproducible. The formula combines the base severity of a finding with a modifier that reflects how exposed the organisation actually is — rewarding strong controls and strong detection, penalising gaps.

Scores, thresholds, and worked examples are published in the app itself at /methodology — available to assessors, auditors, and regulators on demand.

base = Impact × Likelihood (0 – 9)
modifier = 1 + (CW ÷ 3) + (D ÷ 3) (1.0 – 3.0)
residual = round(base × modifier) (0 – 27)
