The Sovereignty Blind Spot
I
Microsoft EU-Compliant ≠ Your Apps Are Sovereign
You correctly validated Microsoft's EU compliance, EU data boundaries, contractual safeguards, and cloud-provider certifications. None of that tells you anything about the third-party apps, AI integrations, automation platforms, and foreign-tenant services your users continuously onboard into the cloud itself. That is where modern sovereignty risk actually lives.
II
Sovereignty Is Not "Traffic Stayed Inside Microsoft"
Real sovereignty is who controls the service, where its cloud communications actually flow, which legal regime applies — and whether the exposure can be defended. A vendor's EU promise is not the same as a continuously classified, jurisdiction-anchored evidence record covering CLOUD Act exposure, FISA 702 reach, and operator-chain visibility.
III
Without Visibility There Is No Defensibility
GDPR Article 28, the Schrems-II ruling, and EDPB Recommendations 01/2020 all require documented evidence of who handles personal data and under whose jurisdiction. If your Microsoft 365 tenant has hundreds of onboarded apps and you cannot classify each one, you cannot defend the exposure to your auditor, your DPO, or your regulator.
